What follows is both scripture and nonsense.

Chapter the First — The Obsession

I 1n the year of our Lord nineteen-eighty-nine, in the great flat kingdom of East Texas, there lived a man called George Cooper. He was a football coach, a husband, and a father — and he was fine. 2Until he tasted the brisket. 3And then he was not fine. He was something else entirely. He was a man with a calling.

"I don't know what she puts in that rub," George said, to no one, to everyone, to the smoker, to God. The smoker did not reply. This is canonical.

Chapter the Second — The Refusal of Meemaw

1Meemaw Constance Tucker, keeper of the Recipe, keeper of secrets, keeper of bourbon, regarded George with the mild contempt she reserved for people who needed things. 2He asked. She refused. 3He asked again. She refused again. 4This happened seventeen times in a single afternoon. 5The seventeenth refusal was delivered without looking up from her crossword. 6This is the most powerful thing any human being has ever done.

The Recipe exists. It is real. It has never been written down. It lives only in Meemaw's mind, alongside her opinions about Elvis and a number of classified personal memories. It will not be shared. It was never going to be shared. George knew this and yet.

Chapter the Third — The Meme Awakening

1In the fullness of time — the year being twenty-and-twenty-three — the Internet beheld S1E7 and understood. 2CapCut editors of great skill took George's energy and made of it a sacrament. 3The deep-fried aura meme spread across TikTok like smoke from a long-burning oak log. 4Ten-hour loops were posted. People watched them. All of them. To the end. 5No one could explain why. This is the nature of the brisket.

Chapter the Fourth — The Wattpad Prophecies

1Then came the Wattpad era. Fan-fiction authors, moved by a hunger they could not name, began to write. 2In these texts — some reaching forty-seven chapters — a different George appears. 3A George who looks at Sheldon and sees tenderness. Not emotional tenderness. 4Brisket tenderness. 5The Cooper family members are, in these texts, described using marinade terminology. 6This is deeply, profoundly, and completely unhinged. 7The lore cannot be stopped. It does not want to be stopped. It is smoking at 225°F and it has been doing so since 2017. 8We are all inside the smoker now.

And lo, they said: is Sheldon the brisket, or is the brisket Sheldon? And there was no answer. And they were at peace.

The Book of Transformations — Extended Non-Canon Lore

What follows is drawn from TikTok prophecy and Wattpad scripture. It has not been verified by CBS. It has been verified by the hunger.

Log I — Sheldon Lee Cooper

Sheldon enters the kitchen at 6:43 AM to explain the thermodynamics of convective heat in slow-combustion cooking environments. He speaks for eleven minutes without pausing. George turns around slowly. His eyes are far away. "Shelly," he says, quietly, with great tenderness and also great hunger, "you've been marinating in that science all morning, haven't you."

Sheldon ran. He is still running. The science of running, he noted while running, involves kinetic energy. He did not share this with George.

Log II — Mary Cooper, née Tucker

George considers it for 0.2 seconds. Mary looks up from her Bible with an expression that has no name in any language. George immediately begins aggressively seasoning a pork shoulder he did not previously plan to season. The pork shoulder will be overworked. It will taste like fear.

Some hungers are sacred. Some hungers are unwise. George Cooper is a man who understands this distinction.
THREAT LEVEL: ✦   OUTCOME: GEORGE STOOD DOWN IMMEDIATELY
Log III — Georgie Cooper (George Jr.)

Georgie walks into the backyard wearing a brown suede jacket and eating a bag of corn chips. He says "whatcha cookin', Dad?" George looks at the jacket. He looks at the smoker. The jacket is the exact colour of rendered brisket at hour fourteen. Neither of them speak of this.

Georgie sold the jacket. He does not know why. It felt right.
THREAT LEVEL: ✦✦✦✦   OUTCOME: CLASSIFIED BY BOTH PARTIES
Log IV — Missy Cooper

Missy, nine years old and armed with perfect comedic timing, looks at her father standing motionless before the smoker at 3 AM and says, "Daddy, are you okay?" George says "I'm fine, baby." He is not fine. He is communing.

She goes back to bed. She will remember this moment for the rest of her life. It is the most George she has ever seen him be.
THREAT LEVEL: ✦ (zero)   OUTCOME: TENDER. NOT IN THE BRISKET WAY.
The Book of George — A Character Study

George Cooper is a man defined by reasonable amounts of quiet suffering and one completely unreasonable obsession with a cut of smoked meat. He is a good man. He is also absolutely unhinged about the brisket. These facts coexist peacefully.

Occupation

Football coach. Father. Husband. Keeper of the Smoker. Seeker of the Recipe. Theologian of Meat (self-appointed).

Known Weaknesses

Brisket. The memory of brisket. The idea of brisket. Meemaw's smug look when she refuses him. Sheldon's voice at 6 AM. Any brown jacket.

Known Strengths

Extraordinary patience. An almost monastic relationship with the smoker. The ability to stand outside at 3 AM communicating silently with wood smoke. Being, fundamentally, a decent man who just really wants the recipe.

Battle Cry

"Connie, I just need one ingredient." †

† — This was said nineteen times over the course of the episode. The nineteenth time, his voice cracked slightly. This is the most devastating moment in television history.

Colophon — Notes on the Making of This Codex

This shrine was built by the internet, for the internet, in the manner of the great illuminated manuscripts — which is to say, with great care about irrelevant details and total indifference to practical utility. 2We are not affiliated with CBS, Warner Bros., or Meemaw Tucker. 3We do not have the recipe. 4No one has the recipe. 5Meemaw has the recipe. 6Meemaw will not share the recipe. 7This is, in a way, what makes it holy.

A Note on the Extended Lore

The transformation lore — wherein a hungry George begins to perceive his family as cuts of meat — is not canonical. It is not endorsed by this shrine, the state of Texas, or the Cooper family. It is, however, inevitable. The internet produces what the internet must produce. We have only documented it. We have done so with the reverence it deserves, which is: a measured amount.
